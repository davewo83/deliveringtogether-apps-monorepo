FeedbackForge Quick Start Guide

What is FeedbackForge?

FeedbackForge is an evidence-based feedback generation tool that helps managers craft personalized, effective feedback messages. Based on psychological research, FeedbackForge tailors feedback to:

  • The recipient's communication style preferences
  • The specific context and purpose of the feedback
  • Research-backed principles of psychological safety
  • Structured feedback models that enhance clarity and impact

Getting Started in 5 Minutes

Step 1: Choose Your Context

  1. Select the Feedback Type:
    • Recognition - Acknowledge achievements and strengths
    • Improvement - Address areas that need development
    • Coaching - Guide progress toward specific goals
    • Developmental - Focus on long-term growth opportunities
  2. Select a Feedback Model:
    • Simple - Basic strengths/improvements format
    • SBI (Situation-Behavior-Impact) - Connect specific behaviors to their outcomes
    • STAR (Situation-Task-Action-Result) - Link actions to achievements within a context
  3. Choose a Delivery Method and Workplace Situation to further contextualize your feedback
Click "See examples" to view sample feedback for each type

Step 2: Understand Your Recipient

  1. Enter the recipient's name and role
  2. Select their Communication Style Preference (DISC framework):
    • Direct (High D) - Prefers concise, results-focused communication
    • Interactive (High I) - Responds to enthusiastic, relationship-oriented messages
    • Supportive (High S) - Appreciates patient, methodical approaches
    • Analytical (High C) - Values detailed, logical explanations
Click "Learn about DISC styles" to see detailed guidance on each style

Step 3: Create Your Feedback Content

  1. Complete the fields for your selected feedback model
  2. Focus on observable behaviors and specific examples
  3. Use the provided help text to guide your entries
  4. Ensure at least one field has content
Click "See examples" to view model-specific examples that illustrate effective feedback

Step 4: Frame Your Feedback

  1. Select Psychological Safety Elements to include:
    • Separate performance from identity
    • Frame as learning opportunity
    • Use collaborative approach
    • Focus on future improvement
  2. Choose an overall Tone that complements the recipient's communication style
  3. Add a Follow-up Plan to show commitment to ongoing support

Step 5: Generate and Refine

  1. Review your settings in the summary
  2. Click Preview Feedback to see and edit the draft
  3. Click Generate Feedback to create the final version
  4. Save as a template for future use (optional)

Working with Templates

Templates allow you to save frequently used feedback configurations:

  1. To save a template, enter a name in the "Save as Template" field when generating feedback
  2. To use a template, go to the Templates tab and click "Use Template"
  3. To delete a template, go to the Templates tab and click "Delete"

Using Examples

FeedbackForge provides various examples to help you craft effective feedback:

  1. Click "See examples" buttons throughout the form to view relevant examples
  2. Use the "Example" buttons to see model-specific feedback samples
  3. In the Templates tab, explore pre-built templates for common feedback scenarios

Psychological Safety

Creating psychological safety is essential for feedback effectiveness. FeedbackForge helps you:

  • Focus on specific behaviors rather than personality traits
  • Frame feedback as opportunities for growth and development
  • Use collaborative language that positions you as a partner
  • Emphasize future improvement rather than dwelling on past issues

Feedback Best Practices

  1. Be specific - Use concrete examples and observed behaviors
  2. Be timely - Provide feedback as soon as reasonably possible
  3. Be balanced - Include both strengths and areas for improvement
  4. Be constructive - Focus on growth and development, not criticism
  5. Be empathetic - Consider the recipient's perspective and needs
  6. Be action-oriented - Include clear suggestions and next steps
  7. Be supportive - Offer resources and assistance for improvement

Remember, effective feedback is a dialogue, not a monologue. Use FeedbackForge to create a foundation for meaningful conversations that drive growth and development.
