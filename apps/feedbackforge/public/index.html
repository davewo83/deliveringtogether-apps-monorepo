Research Basis

Feedback Models

The structured feedback models in FeedbackForge are based on established frameworks proven to enhance clarity and effectiveness:

  • SBI (Situation-Behavior-Impact) - Focuses on specific behaviors rather than personality, reducing defensiveness
  • STAR (Situation-Task-Action-Result) - Provides a structured approach emphasizing the link between actions and outcomes

Communication Style Adaptation

Research shows that personality frameworks like DISC offer valuable insights into feedback preferences. FeedbackForge adapts feedback language to match the recipient's communication style:

  • Direct (D-style) - Concise, results-focused language
  • Interactive (I-style) - Enthusiastic, relationship-oriented language
  • Supportive (S-style) - Methodical, supportive tone
  • Analytical (C-style) - Detailed, logical explanations

Psychological Safety

Psychological safety is essential for creating an environment where feedback is openly received. FeedbackForge incorporates elements that enhance psychological safety:

  • Separating performance from identity
  • Framing feedback as learning opportunities
  • Using collaborative language
  • Focusing on future development

Context Awareness

Research indicates that the setting in which feedback is delivered significantly impacts its effectiveness. FeedbackForge adapts to different contexts:

  • Delivery method considerations (face-to-face, written, remote)
  • Specialized approaches for crisis situations
  • Adaptations for organizational change contexts

About FeedbackForge

FeedbackForge is an evidence-based feedback generator designed to help managers create effective, personalized feedback messages.

Our approach is based on psychological research on feedback reception, including:

  • Psychological safety principles
  • Growth mindset language
  • Communication style preferences
  • Self-concept preservation
  • Contextual factors in feedback effectiveness

By using research-backed approaches to feedback delivery, managers can provide feedback that is better received and more likely to lead to positive behavioral change.
